<WorkProse>
  The true test of our new monorepo architecture came when our GDG Conferences team decided to host
  our first annual AI hackathon: **Mac-a-thon**. Rather than using generic, off-the-shelf event
  software, they outsourced the development directly to my web team. This required extensive
  cross-functional collaboration. By leveraging our shared component library within the monorepo, we
  rapidly scoped, architected, and deployed a dedicated, high-performance portal for the event
  alongside our primary site. It proved the scalability of our infrastructure and delivered a
  cohesive, premium experience for our hackers.
</WorkProse>

<WorkProse>
  Building software is one thing; building an engineering team is another. Over two years, I managed
  and mentored over 15 student developers. The core challenge of leading a university team is
  navigating a massive variance in skill levels—some members were writing their first lines of code

  complex architectural challenges.

<WorkFeatureList>
  <WorkFeature title="Onboarding Beginners">
    I structured beginner-friendly issues to introduce new members to Git workflows, React
    fundamentals, and Tailwind CSS without overwhelming them, giving many their first real exposure
    to shipping production software.
  </WorkFeature>
  <WorkFeature title="Challenging Veterans">
    For our senior members, I delegated complex tasks like optimizing Vercel CI/CD pipelines,

    engaged and growing.
  </WorkFeature>
  <WorkFeature title="Agile Execution">

    we maintained a 100% on-time delivery rate for all club initiatives.
  </WorkFeature>
