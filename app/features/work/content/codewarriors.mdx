<WorkSection>
  <WorkHeader type="THE MISSION" title="Breaking the barrier to entry." />
  
  <WorkProse>
    Learning to code can be incredibly intimidating. For many beginners, the first hurdle isn't the logic itself, but the dry, text-heavy environment in which programming is often taught. At DeltaHacks 9, my team and I wanted to see if we could lower that barrier by borrowing principles from game design.
    
    We set out to build CodeWarriors, a platform that transforms the curriculum into a series of interactive "missions." The goal was to replace the standard, static tutorial with a dynamic experience where progress felt tangible and learning felt like a game rather than a chore.
  </WorkProse>

<WorkSplitContent subtitle="Rapid prototyping for real impact">
  Building a full-scale educational platform in 36 hours required us to be extremely disciplined

<WorkFeatureList>
  <WorkFeature title="Interactive Missions">
    Engineered missions that guide users through fundamental programming concepts using a
    narrative-driven approach, reducing the cognitive load for new learners.
  </WorkFeature>
  <WorkFeature title="Engaging UI/UX">
    Designed an aesthetic that leans into game-like visuals, moving away from the "Integrated
    Development Environment" look to something more approachable and colorful.
  </WorkFeature>
  <WorkFeature title="Concept Validation">
    The core challenge was ensuring that the "game" elements didn't overshadow the actual learning.
    We spent the weekend refining the balance between narrative and education.
  </WorkFeature>
</WorkFeatureList>

<WorkRetrospective title="1st Place Best Overall">
  Being awarded 1st Place Best Overall at DeltaHacks 9 was an incredible honor, especially

  less "scary." It taught me that technical complexity isn't always the goal; sometimes, the most
  impactful thing you can build is a simpler, more empathetic way for people to learn something new.
